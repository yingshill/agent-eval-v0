0. Bottom line

Under the bar Paul locked (quality ≥ 70% / cost ≤ ½), the cheap-model short-list is GLM-4.6 only. But the real product conclusion isn't "swap to GLM or not" — it's how to route:

  • Swap everything to GLM (no routing) = 76% quality / 8.4× cheaper;
  • Route per task (fall back to Claude on hard tasks) = 99% quality / ~2× cheaper.

The latter loses almost no quality and still halves the cost, and it lands right on the ≤½ line Paul set — this is the sweet spot hybrid routing is meant to deliver, and it's how v0.1 moves the project from "can it be replaced" to "how to replace it most cost-effectively."

Quality is the only filter. DeepSeek and Qwen already meet the ≤½ cost bar — what stops them is the 70% quality line. So under this bar the short-list is clean (GLM only), and our external framing is straightforward: filtered purely on quality, not gated by a cost threshold.

2. Per-task tiers: 10 of 14 tasks go to GLM, 4 stay on Claude

We compared GLM against Claude task by task, with the bar set to "GLM's score on this task ≥ 70% of Claude's." The result splits cleanly into two groups:

  • 🟢 10 GLM-safe tasks. GLM meets or exceeds the bar on these, so they can go straight to the cheap model — on financial_stmt, pe_screening, os_log, and tcga GLM mostly scored 100% or higher, and k8s, homework, agora and others also clear the line.
  • 🔴 4 Claude-only tasks. GLM clearly cannot do these, so Claude must be kept: causal_ihdp_ite_estimation (Claude 0.178 / GLM 0.000), crf_sdtm_mapping (0.589 / 0.000), molecular_structure_plausibility (0.500 / 0.167), capacitated_vehicle_routing (1.000 / 0.000).

This shows the right form of routing is not "swap Claude out for GLM wholesale" but "route per task": send what GLM can handle to GLM, fall back to Claude on the hard tasks. This 10-vs-4 split is the first prototype of the routing policy table.

3. Blended savings curve ⭐ (the core conclusion)

Policy Quality = Claude $/task Cheaper
All-Claude (baseline) 0.653 100% $0.439 1×
Pure GLM (no routing) 0.497 76% $0.052 8.4×
Per-task hybrid / verify fallback 0.647 99% ~$0.22 ~2×

In one line: swapping to GLM trades away 24% of quality for 8.4× cheaper; routing per task gives up only 1% of quality and still saves ~2× — hard tasks fall back to Claude, so almost no quality is lost while cost is halved. And this hybrid point lands right on the ≤½ cost line Paul set (0.5× cost / 99% quality), which means his starting bar is an achievable sweet spot under per-task routing, not an arbitrary number.

Routing mechanism: the cheap model runs first → auto-scored → falls back to Claude if it doesn't pass (verify-then-route). Quality matches oracle routing (0.647); cost is slightly higher ($0.22, because fallback tasks are paid for twice).

4. Limitations

  • "99% / 2×" is an achievable upper bound, not a production measurement. It was obtained on the 14 ALE tasks using the real scorer to simulate routing; in production there is no ready-made scorer, so the actual result depends on the accuracy of the classifier and the verify proxy — it will land between "pure GLM at 76%" and "the 99% upper bound."
  • Small sample: common-14; the trend is reliable, but absolute values await a larger N.
  • Edge case: on epidemiology_forecast both models score 0, so it is counted as GLM-safe (both fail → route to the cheap model with no quality loss) — handled as expected and noted.
  • Distribution: still the public ALE benchmark, ≠ BFC's real traffic; the final answer needs real usage data to follow.
